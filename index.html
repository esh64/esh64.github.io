Hello, friend.

My name is Eduardo, I am an Electronic and Computer Engineer and Computer Scientist, and currently a PhD candidate in Computer Science. My doctoral research focuses on developing a general-purpose DataFlow-based computer architecture. My academic journey aligns with my lifelong passion for computers and video games, which I have nurtured since childhood.

I am an avid gamer, both digital and analog, with a special love for retro games, which I also collect. My first experience with video games was playing Donkey Kong Country on my uncle's SNES. However, my deep dive into gaming began when I received my first console, the PlayStation, in 1999, along with computer games running on Windows 98. Since then, I have been passionate about video games. At the end of the year 2000, I also started playing Pokémon TCG, which sparked my interest in card games, board games, and RPG.

My research interests include Machine Learning, Computer Vision, Computer Architecture, and Digital Signal Processing, with applications in Robotics, Biomedical Engineering, Cybersecurity, and Electronic Warfare.

Beyond research, I have extensive experience in programming and software development, primarily in Python, JavaScript, and C. I am also proficient in electronic circuit design, leveraging my skills to develop graphical applications, embedded systems, and Data Science solutions.

Feel free to explore my academic background, published works, and professional experiences on this page. And if you're a retro gaming enthusiast, don't miss the chance to check out part of my collection!
