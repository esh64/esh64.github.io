Eduardo Sperle Honorato

Born in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, I've harbored a passion for computers and video games since childhood. Early on, my keen interest in math and science emerged during elementary school. At the age of 13, I embarked on my programming journey with Python, and my enthusiasm for Computing and Informatics blossomed throughout high school at CEFET from 2012 to 2014.

Uncertain about the path to pursue, in 2015, I enrolled in the BCMT (Bachelor of Mathematics and Earth Sciences) undergraduate program at UFRJ. There, I delved into fundamental subjects encompassing mathematics, geology, astronomy, and chemistry. This experience provided me with the invaluable opportunity to engage in enlightening conversations with various professors, gaining insights into diverse academic domains.

In 2017, I embarked on the journey of the Electronic and Computer Engineering undergraduate program, driven by my enthusiasm for retro gaming and a deep-seated passion for computers. Since then, my academic pursuits have led me to delve into research across Machine Learning, Digital Signal Processing, and Computer Vision. This endeavor has resulted in the publication of 4 conference papers and a journal article, reflecting my dedication to exploring and contributing to these cutting-edge domains.
